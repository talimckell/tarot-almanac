# What Is a Tarot Bearing?

Astrology gives you a birth chart. Tarot numerology gives you one too, built from your birthday instead of the sky. And the heart of that chart, the piece that's most yours, is your Bearing.

Every day, the world gets a tarot card. The same card for everyone, drawn from the date itself. And every day, you get your own card, drawn from your birthday meeting that same date. So there are always two cards in play: the world's, and yours.

Your Bearing is the distance between them.

That distance never changes. Not from the day you were born to today, not from today to the end of your life. The world's card moves, your card moves right along with it, and the space between the two stays exactly the same. That fixed space, the permanent gap between you and the collective, is your Bearing. It's your orientation to the world. The angle you're standing at relative to everyone else. The lens you look through without trying.

## The two sets of cards

Tarot numerology reads the world and reads you on the same three layers.

The world has its set, the collective cards: the card of the year everyone's living through, the card of the month, the card of the day. They belong to all of us at once. When people say a year "had a certain energy," this is that, made specific.

You have your own set, built the same way but turned toward you: your year card, your month card, your day card. Astrology people will recognize the shape of it. Your year card is something like your sun, the core of you. Your month card is more like your moon, your inner life. Your day card is your rising, the way you meet a room.

Two sets, side by side, every single day. And the gap between them is always the same number, year after year. It's built into the arithmetic, and it's easier to show than to describe.

## You can watch the gap hold

Say your birthday is February 16, which makes your Bearing the Moon. Here's your year card next to the world's year card, across different years:

| Year | The world's card | Your card | The gap |
|------|------------------|-----------|---------|
| 1984 | The Fool (0) | The Moon (18) | 18 |
| 2024 | Strength (8) | The Emperor (4) | 18 |
| 2025 | The Hermit (9) | The Hierophant (5) | 18 |
| 2026 | The Wheel of Fortune (10) | The Lovers (6) | 18 |
| 2030 | The Hierophant (5) | The Magician (1) | 18 |

Look down that last column. Both cards change every year. The gap doesn't. The world cycles through the whole deck and you cycle right alongside it, holding the same eighteen steps of distance the entire way. That number, that unchanging eighteen, is the Moon. It's the Bearing.

And it works out to something you can do on a napkin: add your birth month and birth day, wrap it around the wheel of twenty-two cards if it goes past the end, and you've got it. February is 2, the 16th is 16, and 2 plus 16 is 18, the Moon. The reason that shortcut works is the same reason the gap holds. The year cancels out when you measure the distance, and what's left is just your birthday. Your Bearing is your birthday, and your birthday is the gap.

## What the gap means

So your Bearing isn't really a card about you alone. It's a card about you *and the world*, the standing relationship between the two of you. That's why it's an orientation and not a personality test.

A Moon Bearing means you meet the world by feel. You read the undercurrent in a room before anybody says the thing out loud, you trust the hunch, you're at home in what isn't clear yet. That's the gift of the distance you're standing at. The shadow of it is that the same radar can fool you, that a feeling can dress up as a fact. Both are the Moon. A Bearing always holds the whole of a card that way, the brightness and the cost, because it's describing how you're aimed, not whether you're good.

Stand somewhere else on the wheel and it's a different relationship. A Tower Bearing sees what's built on shaky ground and doesn't flinch when it comes down, which steadies some people and unsettles others. A Star Bearing keeps hoping after the storm, the one still pouring water out when everyone else has gone in. None of them is better. They're just different distances from the same center, different angles on the same world.

## How far is your gap

Because the cards live on a wheel, a circle, your gap has a size, and the size is its own kind of meaning.

That short distance is the gap.</figcaption></figure>

Some people stand close to the world's position, a step or two off. They tend to move in step with their moment, fluent in it, rarely the odd one out, though standing that close can make it hard to see your own time clearly. Some stand far, near the other side of the wheel. They often feel a little out of sync, and they see what everyone else is missing precisely because they're standing apart from it. Most people land somewhere in the middle, close enough to belong and far enough to have perspective.

There's one Bearing with no gap at all: the Fool, at zero. Every other Bearing is a distance, a fixed space between you and the world. The Fool is the one with no space. You don't stand back and watch your moment, you're standing right inside it, meeting the world as openly and unguardedly as it meets you. That's the gift of it, a rare kind of belonging, and it's also the catch, because having no distance means having no built-in place to step back and see your moment from the outside. The Fool meets the world fresh every time, no map and no railing, and trusts the ground will be there. It's the rarest Bearing on the wheel. Only a handful of birthdays land there.

Wherever yours falls, it was set the day you arrived and it holds for good. The world keeps turning the wheel. You keep your angle on it.
