## 1. Your Life Path Number and Your Tarot Birth Card
- Status: idea
- Intent: someone who knows (or is looking up) their numerology **life path number**
  and wants the tarot card that goes with it — a huge adjacent audience arriving
  from the numerology side rather than the tarot side.
- Head term: *life path number tarot birth card* (+ "life path number and tarot",
  "what tarot card is my life path number", "tarot card for life path 7")
- Competition & gap: incumbents (numerologist.com, darkforesttarotcards,
  healingthrutarot) publish a rote "life path X → card Y" lookup table and stop. The
  gap we can own is the *mechanism*: why the life-path number and the Major it points
  to are the same numerological move the Almanac already makes, and — the sharp bit —
  why single-digit reduction can only ever reach a handful of Majors while the
  Almanac's mod-22 reads the whole wheel. That correction is the exact argument
  blog-08 and blog-01 already set up, so this is on-spine, not a detour.
- Internal links: **What Is Your Tarot Birth Card?** (sibling term, links both ways),
  **What Is Tarot Numerology?** (why reduction reaches only half the deck),
  **How Tarot Numerology Works** (the wheel of twenty-two), **What Is a Tarot Bearing?**
- Priority: **high** — biggest adjacent search audience we haven't captured, directly
  extends the just-published birth-card post, and winnable because we can answer the
  "why" the lookup-table pages skip.

## 2. Tarot Suits and the Four Elements
- Status: idea
- Intent: a beginner wants to know what Wands / Cups / Swords / Pentacles mean and
  which element each maps to — foundational reference search, very high evergreen volume.
- Head term: *tarot suits meaning* (+ "tarot suits and elements", "wands cups swords
  pentacles elements", "what element is each tarot suit")
- Competition & gap: Biddy, Labyrinthos, Elvi all rank with solid suit-by-suit guides,
  so we won't out-muscle the head term. The content gap that is *ours alone*: the
  Almanac's day card shows a **minor** whose suit is tethered to the day-Major's
  element (Fire→Wands, Water→Cups, Air→Swords, Earth→Pentacles), with the rank from a
  seed formula. No post explains that mechanic yet, and it's load-bearing for the daily
  reading. Frame it as "the four elements — and how a date picks your suit," which no
  competitor can write.
- Internal links: **How Tarot Numerology Works** (day-Major → minor), **What Is Tarot
  Numerology?**, and it becomes a link target for every future day-card / minor post.
- Priority: **high** — the biggest *foundational* gap in the library and the missing
  explainer behind the day reading; slightly less identity-search intent than #1, which
  is the only reason it's second.

## 3. Your Personal Tarot Year Card (any year, from your birthday)
- Status: idea
- Intent: "what's my personal tarot card this year / next year" — evergreen
  calculator intent, distinct from the collective card-of-the-year.
- Head term: *personal tarot year card* (+ "how to calculate your tarot year card",
  "tarot card for my year", "personal year tarot")
- Competition & gap: tarot.com, angelorum, shadowbeartarot all offer a calculator +
  short blurb. Our gap: we compute this deterministically already (the shipped
  year-card calculator), and we can distinguish the **personal** year card (from your
  birthday) from the **collective** year card (shared by everyone) cleanly — a
  distinction the competitors blur. Pairs with, and feeds traffic to, the 2027 post.
- Internal links: **How Tarot Numerology Works**, the 2027 collective-year post (once
  live), **What Is Your Tarot Birth Card?**
- Priority: **medium** — good calculator intent and product tie-in, but overlaps the
  in-flight 2027 scaffold; sequence it *after* 2027 ships so they cross-link.

## 4. Strength, Justice, and the Number Eight
- Status: idea
- Intent: readers puzzled that Strength is 8 in one deck and 11 in another, searching
  for why — plus anyone checking which order a calculator uses.
- Head term: *why is Strength 8 and Justice 11* (+ "Strength Justice tarot swap",
  "Golden Dawn tarot order", "Justice 8 or 11")
- Competition & gap: a few blog posts (3amtarot, clavielle, a Wikipedia paragraph)
  cover the Waite/Golden-Dawn swap. Lower volume, but highly winnable and squarely
  on-brand: the Almanac's math *depends* on the Golden Dawn order (Strength = 8,
  Justice = 11), so we have standing to explain it and to say plainly which order our
  engine uses and why. Natural companion to the 2027 Justice-year post.
- Internal links: **How Tarot Numerology Works** (the order the wheel uses), the 2027
  Justice post, **The Major Arcana in Three Stages**.
- Priority: **medium** — lower demand than #1–#3, but easy to rank, reinforces
  topical authority, and gives the Justice-year post a sibling.

## 5. Tarot and Astrology: Your Zodiac Sign's Major Arcana Card
- Status: idea
- Intent: "what tarot card is my zodiac sign" — very high volume, identity-search.
- Head term: *tarot card for your zodiac sign* (+ "zodiac tarot cards", "major arcana
  astrology correspondences")
- Competition & gap: crowded (tarot.com, Labyrinthos, pagangrimoire, dark forest all
  rank hard) and — the real caution — **off-spine**. It's astrology, not numerology,
  and leaning in risks diluting the deterministic tarot-numerology brand. If done, keep
  it as a bridge that hands the reader back to the birthday-based system ("astrology
  gives you one card; your birthday gives you seven").
- Internal links: **Your Tarot Natal Chart**, **What Is a Tarot Bearing?**
- Priority: **low-medium** — biggest reach of any candidate, weakest brand fit; only
  worth it as a deliberate top-of-funnel reach play, not a core post.

## 6. Tarot Court Cards Explained
- Status: idea
- Intent: beginner reference — what Pages / Knights / Queens / Kings mean.
- Head term: *tarot court cards meaning* (+ "page knight queen king tarot")
- Competition & gap: solid evergreen volume, but the Almanac's engine uses **numbered
  minors only** for day cards — court cards aren't part of the system at all. Off-system,
  so it would read as generic tarot content with no unique angle to defend.
- Internal links: thin — would lean on **Tarot Suits and the Four Elements** (#2) if that ships.
- Priority: **low** — no engine hook; skip unless we deliberately broaden into a general
  tarot-reference cluster.

## 7. Your Tarot Card of the Day (how the Almanac reads a date)
- Status: idea
- Intent: "tarot card of the day" — very high volume, but the searcher usually wants a
  random single-card draw, which is the opposite of what we do.
- Head term: *tarot card of the day* (+ "daily tarot meaning", "how to read a daily card")
- Competition & gap: SERP dominated by free random-draw tools (tarot.com,
  dailytarotdraw). Our angle is the deterministic inversion — everyone alive shares the
  same collective day card, no shuffle — which is genuinely differentiated but fights
  strong intent-mismatch. Reads more like a `/today` landing page than a blog post.
- Internal links: **How Tarot Numerology Works**, **What Is Tarot Numerology?**
- Priority: **low-medium** — strong funnel potential, but the intent mismatch and
  landing-page overlap make it a weaker blog play than #1–#4.
