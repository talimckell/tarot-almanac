## Your Personal Tarot Year Card (find yours from your birthday)
- Status: idea
- Intent: "how do I calculate my personal tarot year card / what's my tarot card
  for this year" — evergreen, recurs every January, and different for every person
  (unlike the collective year card above).
- Head term: "personal tarot year card" (+ "how to calculate your tarot year card",
  "tarot year card by birthday", "my tarot card for 2026")
- Competition & gap: a real evergreen field (yourtango, Mary K. Greer / Archetypal
  Tarot, benebell wen, angelorum) explains the method but stops at "add month + day
  + year, reduce." Almost none tie it to a live engine that also gives you the
  month and day beneath the year, or place the year card inside the Fool's-Journey
  stage it falls in. Our formula (Personal Year = mod22(BM + BD + sumDigits(Y)))
  matches the mainstream method exactly, so we rank on depth + the deterministic
  hook, not a math fight. Strong product-funnel fit (a reusable personal-year
  calculator / paid "year ahead" reading is already a banked idea).
- Internal links: How Tarot Numerology Works (the formula), The Major Arcana in
  Three Stages (which stage your year card sits in), Your 2027 Tarot Year Card (the
  collective counterpart), What Is a Tarot Bearing? (the *lifelong* card vs the
  yearly one); CTA to /today.
- Priority: high — highest-volume evergreen gap we can own, distinct from every
  existing post, and it feeds a real product. Best single *new* topic to green-light.

## Why Some Calculators Give You Two Tarot Birth Cards (and We Give One)
- Status: idea
- Intent: a confused searcher who ran another calculator and got a
  "Personality card + Soul card" (occasionally three) and wants to know why, which
  is real, and how it squares with a single birth card.
- Head term: "two tarot birth cards" (+ "personality and soul card tarot", "why do
  I have two birth cards", "tarot soul card vs personality card")
- Competition & gap: nearly every competitor (BiddyTarot, Tarot School, Labyrinthos,
  tarot.com) teaches the two/three-card Personality/Soul method by reducing the
  date sum. Nobody frames the single deterministic **Bearing** as the cleaner
  answer, or explains that those two cards are just one number shown at two levels
  of reduction. High-intent, high-differentiation, defends the Bearing product
  directly. Watch overlap with post #1's "why your birth card can differ" line —
  this post's distinct angle is the *count* (one vs two), not the *which card*.
- Internal links: What Is a Tarot Bearing?, What Is Tarot Numerology?, The Shadow
  and the Reclaiming (reduction / faces); CTA to /bearing.
- Priority: medium-high — sharp differentiation and a defensible wedge, but narrower
  search volume than the personal year card.

## Tarot Birth Card vs. Your Numerology Life Path Number
- Status: idea
- Intent: readers who've seen tarot birth cards and numerology life-path numbers
  treated as the same thing and want the actual relationship.
- Head term: "tarot birth card life path number" (+ "tarot numerology vs life path",
  "life path number tarot card", "is my tarot card my life path number")
- Competition & gap: the SERP (Numerologist.com, dark forest tarot, freetarot)
  flatly equates birth card with the life-path number and reduces everyone to the
  first nine Majors. Our system reduces mod-22 (Fool = 0, all 22 in play, Golden
  Dawn Strength/Justice order), so we can correct a widespread oversimplification
  while owning the cornerstone "tarot numerology" phrase in the title. Good topical
  authority builder.
- Internal links: What Is Tarot Numerology?, How Tarot Numerology Works, What Is a
  Tarot Bearing?; CTA to /today or /bearing.
- Priority: medium — strong cornerstone-term and corrective angle, but more
  explanatory than product-driving.
