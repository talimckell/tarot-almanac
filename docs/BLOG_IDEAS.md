## Your Tarot Birth Card: The Personality and Soul Cards (and Why the Almanac's Differs)
- Status: idea
- Intent: Someone who's heard they have a "tarot birth card" wants to calculate it, learn how many they have, and know what the Personality vs Soul card means. High-intent, calculator-adjacent.
- Head term: **tarot birth card** (+ "how to calculate your tarot birth card", "tarot personality and soul card", "why do I have two tarot birth cards")
- Competition & gap: Crowded top of funnel — tarot.com, Biddy Tarot, Labyrinthos, Mystic Mondays, HowStuffWorks all rank with calculators and the standard MM+DD+YY+YY method (2–3 cards, Personality reduced to Soul, the 19→Sun/Wheel/Magician edge case). What none of them own is the honest reconciliation: the standard method **includes the birth year and yields 2–3 cards**, while the Almanac's Bearing **excludes the year and yields one**, on purpose. This is the exact wedge blog-01's meta already gestures at ("why your birth card can differ from other calculators"). We can rank for the generic term by teaching the standard method fairly, then route to the Bearing as a deliberate, defensible variant.
- Internal links: what-is-a-tarot-bearing (the payoff), the-tarot-natal-chart, what-is-tarot-numerology, how-tarot-numerology-works (the formula). Out to /bearing and /tarot-birth-chart (the paid product + Google Ads landing).
- Priority: **high** — highest raw volume in the space, the cleanest differentiation wedge the site is literally built to answer, and it feeds both /bearing and the paid /tarot-birth-chart funnel.

## Your Tarot Card of the Year: How to Find Your Personal Year Card
- Status: idea
- Intent: "What's my tarot card for this year?" — wants the calculation (birthday + calendar year) and what the card asks of them. Recurs every January and every birthday.
- Head term: **tarot card of the year** (+ "personal year tarot card", "how to calculate your tarot year card", "tarot card of the year calculator")
- Competition & gap: tarot.com has a calculator; Mary K. Greer / Benebell Wen / Angelorum own the archetypal-tarot framing. Most posts conflate the calculation or skip the collective-vs-personal distinction entirely. The Almanac's engine computes **both** — the collective year card everyone shares and your personal year card from your birthday — which almost no competitor separates cleanly. Owns the "everyone's card vs your card" gap.
- Internal links: how-tarot-numerology-works (the formula), what-is-tarot-numerology, and the 2027 collective post once it's live (evergreen personal companion to the seasonal collective piece). Out to /today and /me.
- Priority: **high** — strong recurring intent, maps directly to the live Personal Year engine, and it's the evergreen counterpart the dormant 2027 post can lean on.

## The Tarot Card for Your Zodiac Sign
- Status: idea
- Intent: "What tarot card is my zodiac sign?" — wants the sign→Major Arcana pairing and a line on why. Very high evergreen volume, astrology-curious audience.
- Head term: **zodiac tarot cards** (+ "tarot card for each zodiac sign", "what tarot card represents my sign", "Aries tarot card / Leo tarot card" long-tail per sign)
- Competition & gap: Very crowded (tarot.com, Pagan Grimoire, Dark Forest, Deckaura) and settled — the twelve pairings come from the Golden Dawn correspondences (Aries→Emperor … Pisces→Moon). Little room to "own" the list itself. The Almanac's authentic angle: it already uses **Golden Dawn order** (Strength 8, Justice 11), so it can tie the astrological correspondence back to the numerology system instead of just listing — the number, the stage, and the sign together. Bridges an astrology-search audience onto the numerology spine.
- Internal links: major-arcana-three-stages (stages give the astro pairings depth), how-tarot-numerology-works. Out to /tarot and the relevant /tarot/[slug] cards.
- Priority: **medium** — big traffic, but crowded and more tangential to the deterministic-numerology core; best as an acquisition bridge, not a cornerstone.

## Tarot Birth Card Compatibility
- Status: idea (product-dependent — see note)
- Intent: "Are we compatible by tarot birth card?" — romantic, high-share, quiz-like intent. Wants to compare two birthdays.
- Head term: **tarot birth card compatibility** (+ "tarot compatibility by birthday", "birth card love match", "[card] and [card] compatibility")
- Competition & gap: Love Project, tarot.com and forums rank; framing is loose and often astrology-borrowed. Gap = a deterministic, honest "here's the fixed distance between two Bearings and what that band means" read, grounded in the Almanac's own math rather than vibes. Banked in memory as the romantic SEO/acquisition half of the compatibility engine.
- Internal links: what-is-a-tarot-bearing, the-tarot-natal-chart. Out to a future compatibility tool.
- Priority: **medium, sequenced** — strong acquisition topic, but the compatibility engine isn't built yet; a post now would be top-of-funnel for a product that doesn't exist. Revisit once the engine (or at least a two-Bearing comparison) ships. Owner has flagged the lineage/family variant as the differentiator over the romantic one.

## Tarot Numerology Number Meanings: What 0 Through 21 Really Mean
- Status: idea
- Intent: Reference/glossary intent — "what does the number on a tarot card mean", walking each Major Arcana number as a numerological idea.
- Head term: **tarot numerology** (the cornerstone) / **tarot number meanings** (+ "major arcana number meanings", "numerology of the major arcana", "what do tarot card numbers mean")
- Competition & gap: Tarot MasterGuide, Tarot Arcana, Numerologist all have long number-by-number guides; the term is our cornerstone so we should own a definitive hub, but the risk is heavy overlap with how-tarot-numerology-works (the formula) and major-arcana-three-stages (the arc). Only worth it framed as a distinct **reference hub** — one anchor per number, linking out to each /tarot/[slug] — rather than another explainer.
- Internal links: how-tarot-numerology-works, major-arcana-three-stages, what-is-tarot-numerology. Out to all 22 /tarot/[slug] Major pages.
- Priority: **low–medium** — best SEO consolidation value as a pillar/hub, but only after the higher-intent posts above; skip if it just restates existing explainers.
